Read carefully!

Often, a forgotten preparatory step is to take a blood test and measure your blood pressure. This is important for two reasons. Some of the results you may need to consult with your doctor and the second reason is that after a few months you will have a starting point to compare what has changed.




To enter ketosis, you normally eat no more than 50 — and as few as 20 — grams of carbs per day for about 30 days.Fat adaptation may start any time between 4 and 12 weeks after you enter ketosis, depending on the individual and how strictly you adhere to the keto diet. Notably, endurance athletes may adapt even sooner.

Keto adaptation

What you need to know before you start.


Important steps to follow in
adapting to ketosis.



Eat lots of fat and fiber


The fuller you feel, the less likely you are to miss your favorite carb-laden foods. Foods made with flaxseeds, for example, are high in both fiber and healthy omega-3 fats.

Increase salt and water intake


Many of the negative side effects are caused by a loss of fluid and electrolytes like sodium (carbs hold on to water, so you'll probably urinate a lot more once you cut them out). To replenish both, drink a cup of water with a half teaspoon of salt stirred into it or a cup of bouillon broth several times a day for a few days.

Go easy with physical activity


As you adapt to a new fuel source, strenuous workouts can further stress your body, so stick to gentle forms of exercise like walking and stretching for a few weeks.

Avoid eating carbohydrates


The most common reason for not getting into ketosis is not cutting back enough on carbs. Restricting carbs keeps blood sugar and insulin levels low Low insulin signals your liver to start burning fat and making ketones

So you are in... What now?


Blood tests


As you entered ketosis, your body began to dra w from its fat stores and dietary fat to convert fatty acids into ketone bodies for energy. Now is a good time to get a blood test. This way you can monitor your progress and check your ketone levels this time. The ketone level indicating ketosis starts at 0.5 mmol / l. “Fat adapted” means that your body has reached a state in which it more effectively burns fat for energy.

Remember about salt and water intake


Dehydration is an increased possibility on keto. “The drastic decrease in carbohydrate intake on the ketogenic diet can cause shifts in your fluid and electrolyte balance.

You can make more exercise now.


Add exercise and keto into your lifestyle to improve your health, then you have more room to experiment with what is best for you.

Remember to get enough sleep


Lack of sleep has been shown to increase sugar cravings and stress levels, which directly affect ketone production.
